Frequently Asked Questions

What is Hyperverse?
A platform that makes it easy for devs to build and consume smart contract primitives as effortlessly as web2 APIs, with monetization and an insurance layer provided by auditor staking.

How can I get involved with the Hyperverse?
Participate in our Hyperhack in November. Hyperhack is a hackathon organized by Decentology. Stay tuned for more information.

How do I register for Hyperhack?
We will be announcing Hyperhack registrations soon! Stay up to date with the announcements by joining our Discord Server.

How much development experience do I need to start building in the Hyperverse?
None! The Hyperverse is meant to onboard web2 developers of all skill levels building within the web3 space.

What are Smart Modules?
They are composable smart contracts that developers will build within the Hyperverse. The Smart Modules will implement blockchain application primitives and have multi-tenancy.

How will monetization work in Hyperverse?
You will have the opportunity to enable transaction-level fees (at a flat rate or based on a percentage of the monetary value of a transaction).

How is the security of the Hyperverse Smart Modules ensured?
All Smart Modules are reviewed and audited. After this approval process is completed, auditors can stake tokens signaling that they trust the code in that Smart Module. Staking holds auditors accountable by being slashed if that trust is lost due to defects or vulnerabilities.

What part will the Hyperverse play in a community-driven DAO?
The Hyperverse must operate within a decentralized network in order to achieve success.

What can I expect to see in Hyperverse in the coming months?
We plan to integrate other blockchains into the Hyperverse ecosystem such as Ethereum and Algorand.

What is composability?
Composability means that the components of the Hyperverse have the capabilities to interact and communicate smoothly.

Decentology


Decentology is the innovative company behind Hyperverse, the open, composable smart contracts marketplace that makes it easy for developers to discover, build and monetize web3 applications. Hyperverse is the evolution of DappStarter, Decentology’s blockchain-agnostic code generation platform for web2 developers.

The Hyperverse uses “composability,” which makes it possible for smart contracts already deployed to a blockchain to interact with each other. Decentology has built a standard set of interfaces that enable developers to easily compose new applications by connecting existing “smart modules” which are single-purpose smart contracts.
